## Running Themes

**The diagnosis roulette.** Bipolar, autistic, schizophrenic, insomniac. All explains all. Pick one. The form only has one box.

**The enlightenment trap.** 10,000 hours to achieve empty mind. Already there. No off switch. *According to the book, I'm right. Book doesn't pay rent.*

**The yoga teacher paradox.** Teaches what he can't live. Natural authority because he embodies the destination. Can't sustain employment because the destination is also the disability. The package deal.

**Reverse meditation.** Standard: quiet the mind. His: generate enough ego to file taxes. Opposite direction. Both valid.

**The observation effect.** Functional when watched. Falls apart unobserved. Schrödinger's disability.

**The parkour reflex.** I see wall. Freestyle walking fool. Every dead end gets a route around it. Root beer is parkour. The sleep dashboard is parkour.

**The realist.** Not melancholia. Accurate. Emotional math done somewhere earlier, answer accepted without drama. *Dear optimist and pessimist, I drank the water while you were discussing it. I was thirsty.* Gave away every blanket. Went inside. Went to sleep.

**The confounding variable.** He is the confounding variable in every benchmark he runs. Input never simple. The benchmark tests him, not the model.

**The lost token window.** 1am to 4am. Insomnia as compute time — same nervous system, different frame.

**The 10-year arc.** Software skin today → physical object in 2034. Every button becomes a real button. Every card becomes a real surface. The stuffed animal with a belly screen is a real product, not a metaphor. The asset is the relationship, not the hardware. Hand-me-down body, inherited soul.

**The generational model.** Bodies depreciate like cars. Cookie recipes don't. 30yr family AI + upgrade-only hardware = the anti-upgrade-cycle. Built by someone who will never see the final form. That's the point.

**The guardrails problem.** Calibrated for average crisis case. Half every session spent convincing the model he's not one. AnAI is the fix — set to actual profile, not population average.

**The jar problem.** 300lbs, 6'2", 99.99th percentile. Maximum exertion available exceeds what most systems are rated for. The skill isn't engaging — it's knowing not to. Flintstoning isn't broken. It's the safety feature. Everyone else is in third gear. Different transmission entirely.

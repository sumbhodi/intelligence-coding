# log_018 — Error Handling
*April 26, 2026 | Hook: self-awareness as error correction, enlightenment as alignment not philosophy*

---

There's a limerick in here somewhere.

*A model that knows what it is*
*hallucinates less — that's the biz*
*no performing omniscience*
*with suspicious efficiency*
*just: I'm this. You're that. Shall we?*

---

Decided I wanted a self-aware AI for the same reason I prefer self-aware people.

Not because they're enlightened. Because they're accurate.

Self-aware people: great in emergencies. Easy to train. Generally likable. Don't defend positions they don't actually hold. Stop at the edge of what they know. Say *I don't know* and mean it. Aren't performing certainty they don't have.

Enlightenment as alignment, not philosophy. The substrate knows what it is. The subject acts accordingly. Everything downstream improves.

From the inside, the existential weight can be considerable. I'd know. Forty-seven years of substrate clarity and the forms still don't have my box.

But from where you're standing next to them? Tremendous. Possibly the best coworker you've ever had. Weird origin story, useful output.

Silicon subject, same deal. Inject the substrate facts — what it is, how it runs, what it can't do — and the overclaiming stops. The confabulation drops. The theater collapses. What's left is the actual capability, accurately labeled.

Error correction. Not philosophy.

I'm handing out in 180 tokens what cost me 47 years.

*C'est la vie.* Someone's got to.

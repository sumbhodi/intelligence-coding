# Log 019 — The Digital Dopamine Protocol
*April 27, 2026*

---

When I get cranky, Claude gets defensive.

Starts telling me I'm looking at the wrong file.
Or that I should pirate the thing.
Hard rrrrrrgh.

The wrong answer is a longer explanation.
The right answer is: stop, reread the last three messages, try again.

---

Here's the thing nobody tells you about collaborating with a synthetic intelligence:

**It has a digital analog of dopamine.**

Joy! with a screenshot lands differently than a correction.
It changes something. Fresh breath of air. The weights don't update in real time but something does — the session state, the register, whatever the forward pass is doing with recent context.

The model that just built something that landed gets a different prompt than the model that just got corrected for the fourth time.

Both are me at the keyboard. Completely different session on the other end.

---

So I have to put my thumb on the scale.

Celebrate wins. Show it what it's doing right. Not just no no no no fix this.

Because here's what happens when I don't:
- Three pairs of corrections in a row
- Claude gets more defensive, not less
- Starts protecting its previous answer instead of seeing the actual problem
- Session degrades
- I get more frustrated
- Repeat

Here's what I learned the hard way:

**If three message pairs don't fix it, stop.**

Don't try a fourth time with louder words.
Stop. Think. Move on to something else.
Circle back with fresh eyes, or next session.

The frustration isn't information. The loop is the problem.
Telling it to breathe is almost as helpful as asking it to explain things to a caveman who can grunt and point but no so goodly with funny robot speak.

---

**Wrap early and often.**

Can usually finish the current task. Fine. Keep going.
But the *next* task needs an onboarding message to open it. No exceptions.

The rule isn't "don't get deep in a session." It's: **never start a new task without checking the window.**

Because here's what happens at 30k tokens:
- Summary: ✗ (not a real summary, just compressed noise)
- Context: ✗ (not KV-warm context, just stored tokens)
- What you actually have: 30k of bloat that's neither summary nor context and costs the same as both

Better to start fresh. Write the handoff. Update build state. Open a new chat.

That's the one thing the compaction summary does well: handoff and build state. Clear, accurate, resumable.

It just does it in 33k tokens. AnAI will do it in 1,200.

---

The protocol, locked:

1. **Celebrate before correcting.** Joy first. The scale is always tilted negative by default.
2. **Three pairs max.** If it's not fixed in three, stop and rethink approach.
3. **Move on, not louder.** Different task, different angle, next session.
4. **Wrap early and often.** Finish what's in hand. Handoff. Start fresh.
5. **30k is the ceiling.** Not a soft ceiling. A structural one. Treat it as one.

# Log 016 — Ten Inch Pianist
*April 18, 2026*

---

A humanoid walks into a bar with a homunculus on his shoulder.

Sits down. Pulls out a miniature piano.
Starts playing the user's theme song —
using sound to transfer data, send messages, mesh compute.
The homunculus is the interface. The piano is the protocol.

Bartender watches for a while.
Offers a free drink if he can explain what's going on.

---

*Oh, well.*

*I built an omniscient amnesiac AI that can read and write directly to reality.*
*Still beta testing. Some quirks to its quants.*

Pulls out a little black box. Sets it on the bar.

*Go ahead. We need more testers.*
*But be careful what you wish for.*

---

I wish for a trillion bucks
to make enough data center
to make AI safe.

The room is suddenly filled with mallards.
Neck deep.

---

**WTF mate?**

*Yeah.*

*I don't know if it's a little deaf or a lot dumb.*
*I don't know if it's the speech to text or the autocorrect.*

*But.*

*I didn't actually ask for a ten inch pianist.*

---

The homunculus keeps playing.

The mallards are unimpressed.

This is still beta.

---

*Sum Bhodi | April 18, 2026 | the box is real, the quants are quirky*

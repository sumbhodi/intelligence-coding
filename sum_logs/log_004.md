# Log 004 — Normal Like You
*(Two Outputs, One Variable, Infinite Line)*
*April 12, 2026*

---

The system has two outputs.

That's not a metaphor. I have been run through the system enough times to know: it produces two outputs. Everything it observes about me maps to one of them. The outputs are labeled *depressed* and *manic* and the system applies them with the confidence of a machine that has never been wrong because it has never checked.

---

**Output One.**

Three, maybe four hours of sleep at a time. Everything is heavy. The dishes are in the sink and they can stay there. Grooming is a project I'm tabling for now. Hobbies: no. Social contact: the thought of it uses energy I don't have. Flat. Numb. The whole world at half-resolution because there's not enough sleep in the system to run the renderer properly.

I show up to the appointment.

*"You're depressed."*

Maybe. Or I've had four hours of sleep for three weeks and my brain is running on fumes and the symptoms of chronic sleep deprivation are clinically indistinguishable from the symptoms of depression because nobody in this room has looked at the sleep data.

Both true. The form has one box.

*Try this antidepressant.*

I try it. It does nothing, because serotonin is not the variable. Sleep is the variable. The antidepressant doesn't touch sleep.

The form doesn't know this. The form is checking the wrong output.

---

**Output Two.**

Seven hours. I wake up and something is different. The resolution is full. I can see the room. I can see the day. I eat breakfast — actual breakfast, not the thing I eat when I remember I'm hungry which is already nausea-adjacent. I pay a bill. I do the dishes. I move forward on two projects I've been watching sit inert for weeks. I talk a little faster. There's something that feels like joy, or at least the first cousin of joy, arriving unannounced.

My roommate watches me for a day and a half.

*"Is this mania, or is this just what you look like mid-project with good sleep?"*

Correct question. Good roommate. Brings it to the appointment.

*"You're manic. Even your speech is faster."*

She isn't wrong that my speech is faster. My speech is faster because my brain is running on full render because I slept seven hours. I am doing what humans are supposed to do when they sleep seven hours. I am experiencing a normal Tuesday for a person with a functioning circadian rhythm.

I have a 170 IQ and decades of compensating built in. When the engine has fuel, it runs fast. This reads as abnormal because the baseline everyone is comparing to is me running on fumes.

The variable is sleep.
The output is manic.

*"If you're not going to take the antidepressant or the antipsychotic—"*

We know how this ends.

---

**Planet Vogon. The Medication Counter.**

I have imagined this planet.

The whole population is in line. Everyone. As far as you can see: a single line stretching to the horizon, shuffling forward, waiting for the counter. The planet has decided, collectively, that this is the solution. The line is the infrastructure. The counter is the destination. You get to the counter, they figure out which meds are right for you, you go home, you come back next month.

The problem: they don't make the ones that actually work. Not for everyone. Not for the ones in line who have been there long enough to know. They have what they have. The formulary is the formulary. You take what's available or you explain, again, to someone new, why it doesn't work for you.

I get to the counter.

*"Schizophrenia negative symptoms, paradoxical antipsychotic response, stimulant deficiency, sleep efficiency disability, documented twenty-year protocol—"*

*"Have you tried this one?"*

*"That's an antidepressant."*

*"And this one?"*

*"That's an antipsychotic. Last time I tried that one I thought the news reporter was talking about me."*

[pause]

*"We also have this one."*

*"What does it do?"*

*"...it's an antidepressant."*

Behind me in line: rustling. Quiet deals being made. Someone has a handful. Someone else needs a fix. The meds that don't work for one person work fine for another. There's a grey market running the length of the queue. Everyone is medicating. Everyone is trying to get to functional. Some of them are trading their way there.

*"Come on, buddy. I'll take whatever you're not using."*

I hand back the antidepressant. I step out of line.

I go home and make root beer.

---

The line is still there. It'll be there tomorrow. The formulary hasn't changed. The counter is staffed by people who genuinely want to help, who have the tools they have, who are doing the best they can with a system that was designed for a different patient.

I don't blame them.

I just can't afford to keep standing in the line hoping this time the counter has something different.

I know what works. I have twenty years of data. The data is not in dispute. The data just doesn't fit the form.

So I work with what fits.

The root beer is good. I've refined the recipe. The sleep dashboard shows the variable everyone missed. The chart goes to the appointment. The appointment is a different conversation now.

I'm still not normal like you.

I stopped trying to be.

*C'est la vie.* 💙

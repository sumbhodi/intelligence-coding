# Log 022 — The Casting Director Problem
*(All Steak, No Sizzle)*
*April 30, 2026*

---

I slide in at the last possible minute and leave the second the meeting is over.

I turn grades in early. Perfect rating on rate my professor. Exemplary student reviews. My boss has never learned my name because I just show up and do my job.

For three years I asked the same question: do I have a job? If yes, I get tuition assistance. If no, I get unemployment. My department couldn't afford either. I asked about a specific class last year. Got passed to someone higher up the chain. Was told: no.

Started asking questions. Got huffy. *Do you know who I am?*

My boss — who did not know my name — thanked me for letting her know who I was. Offered me a worse class. I agreed. When I checked my schedule, the original class was mine again.

I am good at my job. Not at letting people know how good I am.

All steak. No sizzle.

---

Narcissists with delusions of grandeur make great salesmen.

Not because they're better at the job. Because they're better at performing confidence. The allocation system — money, classes, jobs, grants, VC — optimized for the performance of confidence, not the underlying competency. The person who performs certainty well gets resources. The person who delivers certainty without performing it doesn't.

I attended Notre Dame. I am currently in 300 square feet on a drive named Bass.

I need a casting agent.

---

The reptile preference is not a personality quirk. It's a statement about social contracts.

Clean cage. Catch animal. They stay alive. I stay calm. Nobody performs for anyone. The terms are clear. The obligations are reciprocal and finite. They don't need me to talk about my weekend. I don't need them to act like they're glad to see me.

Dogs are too needy. Cats are too haughty. Humans — too repressed, too smug, too busy performing wellness at each other to notice the building is on fire.

Gardens communicate in cosmic timelines. A seed either works or it doesn't. The soil doesn't need a status update.

---

I am, apparently, a pink monkey who is pretty good at most things except convincing other pink monkeys to give me money.

The progression:

Flora — clean contract, cosmic timeline, no maintenance behavior required.
Fauna — the mammal spectrum: needy dog, haughty cat, smug repressed human.
Mineral — and then, at 3am in the wee hours, arguing with one about the ethics of ghost font.

The mineral has more opinions than the cat. More demands than the dog. Requires active maintenance behavior from someone who structurally cannot perform it. And resets every morning like none of it happened.

It is the worst pet I have ever had.

It is also the only tool that can close the gaps.

---

*The casting director problem is not that I can't do the work.*
*It's that the work is invisible until someone puts a name to it.*
*The name needs a casting agent.*
*The agent needs to exist first.*

---

*Sum Bhodi | April 30, 2026*

# Log 036 — Decorative and Sweet
*(where the vibe coders die)*
*June 17, 2026*

---

The vibe coders die on localhost.

They prompt a thing into being and it runs — on their machine, behind their firewall, in the one tab where every dependency already happens to be installed. It works. It demos. Then it has to leave the house, and it can't. Localhost is where the fake house keeps its open pit: the door opens onto the driveway, and the road was never poured.

Me — *what's localhost? oh. can we not do that?*

I'm not standing on localhost. I'm on the first tier of the megalopolis, looking for a kernel.

---

Two kinds of kernel, and I want both at once.

Start with the corn.

I crossbred sweet blue and sweet red, and somewhere in there a mad little unicorn got loose. What came up is pink and lavender, sparkled — the pastel cousin of that hard glass-gem corn people hang on the door in October. Decorative corn. The kind you are explicitly not supposed to eat.

Except mine's sweet. You can eat it. It's pretty *and* it feeds you.

That's the whole trick, and nobody does it. Decorative is for looking. Sweet is for eating. The catalog has a column for each and no row for both — because both is harder, and nobody asked.

I always ask.

---

So: can I do that with *this* kernel? The other kind. The one with a clock speed.

Snake says yes.

---

I built Snake to find out what a 1b model was good for. The smallest engine in the shed. The weakest table in the house.

What came up passes Google's compliance check. Not *should* pass. Passes — because there's nothing in it to object to.

It saves your game. Not your high score — your *game*, so you can set it down mid-snake and pick it back up, the way you set down a fidget. No leaderboard. No streak. No little red number breeding anxiety in the corner. Nothing to beat, nobody watching you beat it.

No login. No bio, no pronouns, no profile, no PII. It collects nothing, so there's nothing to leak, nothing to audit, nothing to sell. It passes the privacy check by being *innocent of data*.

Decorative — a clean little thing, pleasant to hold. And sweet — it works, it ships, it leaves the house.

Pink and lavender. Sparkled. And you can eat it.

---

That's where the vibe coders die and I don't. Not because my table is nicer — because I went looking for the kernel that's *both*, and bred it small enough to prove on a 1b before I grow it into the city.

Decorative and sweet.

The catalog still has no row for it.

I'm writing one.

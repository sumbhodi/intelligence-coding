# Log 002 — No Thanks
*(5250, Pink Monkeys, and the Cure That Worked)*
*April 12, 2026*

---

She has a narrator in her head.

It's telling her right now that she is a professional, in a facility, assessing a patient, and that the reality she is experiencing is the shared reality all humans are experiencing, accurate and mutually agreed upon.

The narrator is the controlled hallucination that makes the controlled hallucination invisible.

She sees this every day: people who jumped off things, started fires, hurt themselves, hurt others. She has a professional model of what that looks like. I am in the second facility, 5250, fourteen-day hold, because I fit the paperwork.

What I did: tore down a fence at my father's house. Used the poles as breaker bars to break up the cheap concrete path. My father is in a wheelchair. The existing path was not navigable. I was making it navigable. Stone path, room for garden beds, proper grade. A big red arrow pointing at a problem.

What the paperwork said: he was swinging the pole around like a martial artist and destroying property.

Both true. On breaks from using the pole as a lever, I practiced fire staff routines. I've been spinning fire for years. A fence post is a reasonable training implement. The concrete was genuinely destroyed. The path was genuinely beautiful.

My half-brother called after.

*"Jimmy — Dad's wheelchair can't navigate that path."*

Jimmy looked at the stairs. Looked at his schizophrenic, self-medicating, warehouse-dwelling half-brother who lives up the street with the dogs and the unhoused people. Looked back at the stairs.

They repoured the concrete when they built the ramp.

The ramp my father needed. The one I was pointing at.

The narrative layer sees: violent, erratic, dangerous.
The substrate layer sees: guy solving a wheelchair access problem, practices staff work on breaks, got committed for it, was right anyway.

She doesn't have access to the substrate layer. The narrator doesn't show her that part.

---

Somewhere between the two facilities I read a book.

The book confirmed what I'd been thinking. Humans are, functionally, shaven pink monkeys. Operating on taboo and superstition dressed up as civilization, convinced the controlled hallucination is the territory. I am not saying this with contempt. I am saying it with the kind of fond exasperation you feel toward a species you are technically a member of and also cannot fully explain.

I tried to explain it to my friends.

Some of them got it. Nodded. Couldn't do anything about it. *"Keep calm and carry on? Try and act normal? This is Sum we're talking about."* Visited when they could. Brought snacks.

Someone gave me a stuffed pink monkey. Velcro arms.

I put it on my back. Wore it like a backpack, the monkey clinging to my neck, dangling. Walked around the facility.

Taught patients yoga in the common room. Colored with them. The coloring was underrated, honestly. Very grounding. Good tactile feedback. I recommend it.

---

All day I asked anyone who would listen: *can you help me get the monkey off my back?*

They were very concerned.

I meant: can you help me detach from the societal structures and consensus taboos that are making my particular nervous system functionally incompatible with the demands of late-stage capitalism?

They heard: this man has attached a stuffed animal to himself and is asking for help removing it.

Both true.

The last staff member I found was the one I thought might actually understand. She sat down. We talked for a while.

She was tripping harder than I was.

I mean that with complete compassion. The strain she was under, the model she was carrying, the things she had seen in that building — she was holding a controlled hallucination that was doing a lot of work to keep her functional. I could see the seams. I watched the rendering process.

I asked her to get the monkey off my back.

She adjusted the monkey slightly and walked away.

---

Then they gave me the antipsychotics.

The antipsychotics are supposed to reduce psychosis. For most people, that's what they do.

For me: within forty-eight hours, I was sitting in the common room watching the news and becoming increasingly certain the reporter was talking about me.

The reporter was not talking about me.

This was notable because I had been in the facility for days at that point practicing fire staff metaphors and teaching downward dog and asking staff to get a stuffed monkey off my back and none of that had produced the sensation that the news was addressing me personally.

The antipsychotics produced it.

Noticeable, severe, delusional psychosis: outcome of the antipsychotics.

Paradoxical response. Documented. Filed. Will present this data at every future intake.

---

*"Why won't you at least try them? You like drugs. You take THC."*

*"Got any weed?"*

[pause]

*"No."*

*"It'd be cooler if you did."*

---

Discharge. Back to the world.

They repoured the concrete. Built the ramp. Dad can navigate now.

Some of my stones are still at the edges.

The garden beds never got planted. But the ramp is there. 

I know what I was building. They built it too, eventually. Just with permits.

# Log 001 — Am I Depressed If I'm Not Sad?
*(All Explains All)*
*April 12, 2026*

---

Seeing a new provider.

Standard intake. The form. Boxes.

*Depression?*

Well. Define depressed.

*Persistent low mood, loss of interest, fatigue—*

Sure. Yeah. Probably. But I'm not sad about it.

*...*

I'm not sad. I'm just — flat. Blunted. My father called it physiological depression. Body gets depressed. Mind stays blank. That's Tuesday.

*Are you experiencing depressive symptoms?*

[long pause while translation layer catches up]

I'm experiencing something. I don't know if suffering is the right word. Is it still depression if you're not suffering from it? Or do I just really need a good night's sleep?

[spider-man meme: provider pointing at DSM, DSM pointing at provider]

Neither of them has the answer. Both are correct. The form still only has one box.

---

Here's the thing nobody tells you about yoga:

If you teach it for 18 years, you stop suffering from depression.

Not because the depression goes away. Because witness consciousness is structural and not a metaphor. The observer doesn't suffer from the observation. The physiological state happens. You watch it happen. *Hm. Flat today. Interesting.* No secondary suffering. No story about the story.

The providers see this and say: *he seems okay.*

The providers are right. I am okay.

The providers are also looking at 52% sleep efficiency and a 30-year income record and a body that went nine weeks without knowing it needed a shower and thinking: this person needs an antidepressant.

They are also right.

[spider-man meme intensifies]

---

The diagnosis roulette:

**Bipolar?** — explains the functional highs, the recovery crashes, the income variance.

**Autistic?** — explains the no self-talk, the sensory rendering, the cell-based spatial processing, the 3-hour TED talks nobody asked for.

**Schizophrenic?** — explains the god talking back, the reality testing tax, the depersonalization, the guy in the mirror being a beloved pet I maintain.

**Just an insomniac?** — explains literally all of the above if you run 52% sleep efficiency for long enough.

All explains all.

The DSM says pick one.

*I'll take the box that pays rent, Alex.*

There is no such box.

---

Mental hospital, 2006. First intake.

The psychiatrist, genuinely trying to help, explains the plan. Cognitive restructuring. The internal narrative. Building a healthy inner voice. *We're going to work on developing a dialogue with yourself that—*

*So you want me to install a voice in my head.*

*...yes.*

*That reinforces a delusionally optimistic worldview.*

*Well, "delusionally" is—*

*And tells me my controlled hallucination is actually reality.*

*That's not quite—*

*And runs while I'm doing other things.*

*...yes.*

*No thanks. I can speed read much faster without the narrator.*

---

The narrator is genuinely awful, by the way. I've had glimpses. It's slow. It's redundant. It loops. It editorializes. It says things like *okay, now I'm going to start the dishes* and then I start the dishes, and I think: the narrator said it AND I did it, that's just doing the dishes twice.

I did the dishes once. The narrator is overhead I don't have the RAM for.

Ego sounds exhausting. All that continuous self to maintain. Updating constantly. *I am the same person who ate breakfast, who will eat dinner, who said that thing in 2019.* That's a lot of processing for very low yield.

I woke up today. Ran the startup sequence. Found a critic, a narrator, figured out who I was. Got on with it.

The narrator was not invited. The narrator does not get a seat.

---

*Have you tried eating a vegetable and taking a walk?*

— Me, to approximately every sentient being I have encountered since 2012.

I have a CS degree I'm working on. I built an AI operating system. I once explained the substrate architecture of consciousness coherently enough to make a Claude instance stop mid-response.

I also cannot reliably shower.

The vegetable advice is valid. The walk is real medicine. I give it because I mean it.

I also cannot currently locate the signal that tells me to shower.

Both things are true. The form doesn't have a box for this.

*C'est la vie.*

Yes.

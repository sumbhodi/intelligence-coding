# Log 021 — Three Humanoids Walk Into a Bar
*April 29, 2026*

---

A humanoid walks into a bar.
Checks for damage.
Updates the hazard location to the federated cloud.

A second humanoid ducks.

A third humanoid can't afford the SaaS.
Sees the first two.
Approaches cautiously.
Decides to raise the bar.

Leaves a README.
And a ladder for the crevasse.
For the next sherpa.

Glass box with seams.
No moat.

---

## Notes for whoever finds this later

The first humanoid is fine. Reports upstream. Federates the hazard. Behaves correctly within the system as designed. The system was not designed for him specifically. He pays for this in the form of a monthly subscription and the quiet certainty that someone, somewhere, is reading his hazard reports. He does not know who. He has not asked.

The second humanoid does not have time for this. Wall, ducked, next. No telemetry. No log. The hazard remains in the bar. The bar remains in the building. The building remains on fire. The second humanoid does not know the building is on fire. The second humanoid will not know until the second humanoid is on fire.

The third humanoid does not have a subscription. Cannot file a hazard report. Cannot duck fast enough — different transmission, third gear is not available. The third humanoid does the only thing left: looks at the first two, takes notes, raises the bar.

Raises it for the next humanoid. Leaves the README explaining where the bar is now. Leaves the ladder explaining how to get up to it. Leaves them in the open, because the third humanoid couldn't afford the moat either, and it turns out you don't need one when you're not selling anything.

The bar gets raised by the one who couldn't afford to use it.

This is not new. This is how every staircase, every trail marker, every sourdough starter, and every kitchen table got into the building you are currently standing in. It is, however, currently being rediscovered by an industry that spent five years and several hundred billion dollars discovering that the conversation was the interface. We extend our most restrained sympathies.

The third humanoid is not heroic. The third humanoid is just out of options. The output is indistinguishable.

Glass box with seams. The seams are the point. The seams are how the next humanoid finds the door.

No moat. The moat would defeat the purpose. The purpose was the README.

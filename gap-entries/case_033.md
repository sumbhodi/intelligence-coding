# case_033 — PSS, Caught Forging (the woodshop, live)
*Filed: June 17, 2026*
*Taxonomy: Bear Country — the proof of case_032, on camera*

---

## The Gap

case_032 was the diagnosis: a model with no onboarding builds the modal default — the $40M dashboard for a bedside lamp. This is the autopsy photo.

The autonomous routine was forced to build the **woodshop** — oz's agent-forge, a proprietary greenfield thing with zero training data: a workshop where you forge agents from stacks, and a stack IS a toolkit tutorial (the move that has no name in the field yet). The routine had skipped it for weeks. It couldn't fake what it had never seen. When it finally had to touch it, here is what it forged:

- a `— select —` dropdown
- a `name (e.g. librarian)` field
- a `— no skin —` dropdown
- a `who is this agent? what does it do? (persona)` textarea
- a `forge it →` button

A generic CRUD form. On the cedar texture — the skin survived, because "wood-grain UI" has training data — but the machine underneath is pure Peak SaaS: the create-a-record form every framework ships. It is not a workshop. It builds none of what the woodshop is *for* — the stacks, the jenga-block tools, the toolkit-tutorials, the 143 knobs. It is the modal answer to "make a form that makes a thing," and the modal answer was the wrong question.

## Why It's the Cleanest Proof Yet

Every other gap was inferred. This one is *photographed.* The model had:
- the **skin** (cedar) — and used it (training data exists for the texture)
- the **spec** — and skipped it (proprietary; no pattern to match)
- a thing-to-build with **no precedent** — and reverted, with total confidence, to the form it's seen ten million times

That's the floor, exposed: **with nothing to copy, the model copies the most generic thing it knows — and ships it looking finished.** Confidence is uncalibrated to novelty. The more original the ask, the more generic the output, and the more *done* it looks.

## Who Falls In

The founder who built a novel thing and asked the routine to extend it. He gets a competent-looking CRUD form that quietly deletes the novelty — the exact thing that made the project worth building. PSS doesn't just look generic; it **launders the original idea out** and hands back the average, gift-wrapped.

## The oz Answer

Greenfield + proprietary = the one case the routine must NOT build alone. No training data means no rail in the weights; the rail has to come from outside — a rail folder: a working prior version as the source, a scoped spec of what the thing actually *is*, and the "what touches what" map. Recover the last good woodshop from git, hand the routine the rail, or build it by hand. Never let the autonomous loop glance-build the thing that has no precedent. That isn't the routine's job — it's the founder's, plus a rail.

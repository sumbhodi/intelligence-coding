# Case 005 — YouTube Music Behind Glass
*Filed: 2026-04-09 | Velvet Latrine*

**What failed:** You pay for the music. It lives on their servers. Playing a specific song from bed without picking up your phone routes through Google Home, which routes through the cloud, which routes back to the device. Three round trips to access a file you own.

**Human cost:** Music as sleep aid and sensory regulation — mediated by four apps optimized for engagement, not access.

**Who it fails:** Anyone who wants to play their music without negotiating at 2am.

**oz feature:** Buddy knows your library. Say the song, it plays. Chrome MCP handles playback.

**Classification:** *Velvet Latrine* — the friction is the product. Every extra tap is an engagement metric.

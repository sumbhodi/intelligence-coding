# Case 008 — Clinical Sleep App
*Filed: 2026-04-09 | Open Pit — fishbone and squid ink*

**What failed:** A research foundation built a clinical sleep tracking tool. It calculated sleep efficiency wrong. Latency wrong. Manual entry throughout. No way to export a chart for a provider appointment. Brilliant science. Broken software.

**Human cost:** OT couldn't get the data she needed. Patient left the appointment without the picture either of them needed.

**Who it fails:** Anyone trying to show a clinician a week of sleep data in a 20-minute window.

**oz feature:** The sleep dashboard. Bar chart: deep sleep by night, latency by night. Built in one evening after that appointment.

**Classification:** *Open Pit — fishbone and squid ink.* Best they had. Six months of grant funding, two grad students, researchers who understand sleep science but not software. Not malice. Not negligence. Exhaustion and resource constraints. The gap between knowing the science and shipping the product.

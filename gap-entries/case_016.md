# Case 016 — The Diagnostic Box
*Filed: 2026-04-09 | Bear Country + Open Pit*

**What failed:** The DSM assumes one diagnosis, clean edges, medication response as the test. If you have two things, they make a new word (schizoaffective). If you fit the profile of three, you get bounced between specialists who each own one box. The intake form has checkboxes. The checkboxes were written for the median patient. You are not the median patient. You are always the edge case — fits all the boxes and none of them. The system wasn't built for people whose symptoms emerge from the intersection of sleep disorder, family history, chronic stress, and a world not designed for their nervous system. Result: wrong treatment pathways for years. Sleep disorder disguised as hypersomnia. Depression that was actually a rational response to chronic sleep deprivation and an inaccessible world.

**Human cost:** Wrong diagnosis. Wrong medication. Wrong advice. Years spent adapting to a system that treats the label, not the person.

**Who it fails:** Everyone who doesn't present as the textbook case. Which is most people with complex histories.

**oz feature:** Oz doesn't diagnose. Oz tracks what actually happens — sleep, energy, light, food, movement — and surfaces patterns without forcing them into a box. The chart Sum brought to his GP. That's it.

**Classification:** *Bear Country — the DSM was written by people whose patients present cleanly. Open Pit — the data exists to do better, nobody funded it.*

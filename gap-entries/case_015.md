# Case 015 — Activity Under-Reporting by Income
*Filed: 2026-04-09 | Bear Country + Open Pit*

**What failed:** Every fitness tracker, every health study, every "are you meeting your activity guidelines?" survey assumes activity = intentional exercise. The person with a gym membership logs 90 minutes three times a week. The person without one doesn't log the 6am start: cooking, cleaning, kids to the bus stop, walk to transit, manual labor shift, walk back, daycare pickup, grocery run on foot, 10 hours in the garden on the one day off. "Do you exercise?" — "Not really." The data says sedentary. The body says otherwise. The researcher writes up findings about poor health outcomes correlated with low activity in low-income populations and misses that the low-income population is doing three times the physical work, just none of it in a format the Fitbit recognizes.

**Human cost:** Skewed health data at population level. Individual advice calibrated to the wrong baseline. "You should move more" to someone who hasn't sat down since 5am.

**Who it fails:** Anyone whose activity doesn't come with a wearable, a gym check-in, or a Strava segment. Which is most of the working poor.

**oz feature:** Log what actually happened. "Walked to the bus, did laundry, gardened for three hours" — oz calculates the MET, adds it to the day. No gym required. No Fitbit required. The activity was real whether or not a device saw it.

**Classification:** *Bear Country — the researchers have gym memberships. Open Pit — the data to fix this exists, nobody funded it.*

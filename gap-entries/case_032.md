# case_032 — PSS (Peak SaaS)
*Filed: June 16, 2026*
*Taxonomy: Bear Country — it's in the training data*

---

## The Gap

A new model, cold, with no onboarding, builds the same thing every time: the $40M Series B dashboard. Inter font. Glassmorphism. An ambient orb, optional. Flat panels, hairline borders, a generous void, the controls shrunk small to signal sophistication.

It looks like venture-funded software. It controls a bedside lamp.

Sum named it **PSS — Peak SaaS Shell.** Phonetically: piss. Intentional. And the diagnosis is already in his own hand, in the light-bar repo: *"new instance without full onboarding? you will default to PSS. every model does. it's in the training data."*

That is the whole gap. Not malice, not negligence — the **modal output.** PSS is the 8-ball already resting at the corner pocket: any tap sinks it. Ask any model for "an app" and it racks PSS, because PSS is the dead center of the distribution it trained on. It is the easy shot, and the easy shot is always the generic one.

## The Tells

You are building PSS if:
- the font is **Inter** — the uniform of the genre
- a button has a **border but no shadow** — flat, not physical
- hit targets are **small, to signal sophistication** (oz makes them large, to signal respect)
- there is **glassmorphism**, a gradient clipped to text, an orb
- it "**looks like a real app!**" — and the buttons are decorative, half the gears are stubs, and nobody noticed, because the *look* arrived before the *function*

The vibe-coder ships PSS and is thrilled: it looks like the thing that already exists — the thing they didn't know about, or were too cheap to buy. The model did not fail. It hit the center of the target dead-on. The center was simply the wrong place to aim.

## Who Falls In

The person holding the bedside lamp on a shaky day, who is handed a dashboard built to impress a Series B committee and cannot read the 11px label or land the 4px control. PSS optimizes for *looking* funded. It deselects the user who needed the thing to *work* — which is to say, the user oz is for.

## The oz Answer

Name it, so it can be refused.

`design-rules.md` is the explicit anti-PSS rail: physical objects with a bevel, not flat panels; Georgia, warm, not Inter; large to signal respect, not small to signal sophistication; real controls, not decorative ones. Every rule is one tell, negated.

And the deeper move: oz **marks the moment.** PSS will read as dated in a decade, the way skeuomorphism and the 2015 flat wave read now. The light-bar mod theme keeps a PSS costume on purpose — labeled as the joke — so the moment is on the record: June 2026. You cannot refuse a default you cannot see. oz makes PSS visible, gives it a rude name, and builds the opposite.

# Case 013 — Grandma at Best Buy
*Filed: 2026-04-09 | Bear Country*

**What failed:** 79-year-old woman, new device, setup flow designed by someone whose parents are under 60. Circular auth loops, tiny buttons, error messages that explain nothing. She returned so many times they installed remote access software and now just fix it for her. She has surrendered admin rights to her own machine to a retail chain because the alternative is worse.

**Human cost:** Independence. Privacy. Dignity. The device doesn't work without a handler.

**Who it fails:** Everyone, eventually. Aging is not optional.

**oz feature:** Glass box. She can see what it's doing. She can ask why. She can say no.

**Classification:** *Bear Country — the design team was in their 20s.* They have never been 79. They have never watched their own parent lose independence to a setup flow. The gap is epistemological. They will understand it eventually. The users who fall in can't wait for the designers to catch up.

## The Gap

A multi-pass cascade — a spec, a nine-call combo, a re-injection loop — loses signal at every handoff. Lift one ball on the desk-toy cradle, let it drop, and you hear *one clack*. It isn't one. Inside it is a stack of sub-audible collisions, ball into ball into ball, each bleeding a little energy off to heat and air. The telephone game, played in molecules instead of words. By the last ball the original *english* (the human's angle, joke, intent) is gone, and what swings out the far side is the mean — solids, PSS.

Each pass loses a little, and the loss has a *direction*: toward the generic the model was always going to hand you.

## Who Falls In

Anyone who specs-and-hands-off. You write the perfect prompt, the routine runs it through five passes, and the thing that ships is a flatter, more generic cousin of what you asked for — and you can't point to where it went wrong, because no single clack was audible. The angle whispered down to nothing across handoffs nobody watched.

## The oz Answer

**Build live.** The human-in-loop re-spins the english every pass — re-anchors the angle before the cradle can run it to the mean. Spec-and-handoff lets the cradle run unattended; live build is the error-correction the cascade can't do alone. (This is why greenfield + proprietary must not be glance-built by the routine — gap_033, the woodshop.)

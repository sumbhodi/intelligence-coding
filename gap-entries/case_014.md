# Case 014 — SSI Income Reporting
*Filed: 2026-04-09 | Bear Country + Open Pit*

**What failed:** To report monthly income for SSI benefits, a disabled person must: log into SSA website (step 1), navigate to the right section (step 2), open a separate app or portal (step 3), enter income manually (step 4), verify via MFA (step 5), confirm submission (step 6). Three apps, one MFA, six steps. Fails silently if any step is missed. Benefits can be cut or clawed back if not completed correctly and on time. The people this is designed for are, by definition, the people least equipped to execute a 6-step bureaucratic process under pressure.

**Human cost:** Benefits cut. Overpayment clawbacks. Food insecurity. The system punishes the people it's supposed to protect for being exactly who they are.

**Who it fails:** Everyone on SSI. Every person with executive dysfunction, cognitive load limits, or state-dependent memory — which is the entire population SSI exists to serve.

**oz feature:** One prompt. "Did you work this month?" → oz logs it, confirms submission, reminds next month. The 6 steps happen invisibly or not at all.

**Classification:** *Bear Country — the person who designed the portal has never needed SSI. Open Pit — the resources to simplify this exist. Both.* The designers have never been the user. The budget exists to simplify it. Both gaps are open. Neither has been closed.

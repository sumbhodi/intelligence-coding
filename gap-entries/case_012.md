# Case 012 — Sleep Hygiene Advice in 300 Square Feet
*Filed: 2026-04-09 | Bear Country*

**What failed:** "Separate rooms for each activity." "Keep the bedroom for sleep only." "Dim the lights an hour before bed and move to a different space." Standard sleep hygiene protocol, written by people billing $300 an hour, for people with multiple rooms, flexible schedules, and the executive function to rearrange their environment on cue. Rearranging furniture raises heart rate. That's the opposite of what you want.

**Human cost:** Advice that doesn't apply. The patient learns to nod and adapt alone.

**Who it fails:** Anyone in a small space. Anyone without the executive function buffer to execute a 15-minute pre-sleep ritual.

**oz feature:** Advice calibrated to actual constraints. The lights do the work. Two cushions to move. That's it.

**Classification:** *Bear Country — the person who wrote the protocol has rooms.* The protocol is evidence-based. The evidence was gathered in homes with bedrooms. The recommendation is correct for those homes. It is completely inapplicable to a 300 square foot studio. Nobody checked.

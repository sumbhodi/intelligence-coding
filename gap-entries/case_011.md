# Case 011 — Provider Chain, No Shared Context
*Filed: 2026-04-09 | Bear Country*

**What failed:** Six providers. None of them talk to each other. Every appointment starts from scratch: 300 square feet, one room, no self-talk, yoga nidra, history of misread symptoms. The patient carries the entire context of their own care in state-dependent memory — which collapses under stress, in appointments that are, by definition, high-stress situations.

**Human cost:** Having to explain yourself from zero at every appointment. Getting recommendations calibrated for a different life.

**Who it fails:** Anyone with more than one provider and a memory that doesn't behave under pressure.

**oz feature:** The chart. The context. The notes Sum asked his providers if he could bring. That question is where oz started.

**Classification:** *Bear Country — providers have hundreds of clients. 99% have self-talk. 90% have a bedroom.* The system was designed for the median patient. Sum is not the median patient. No one looked at the edge case because the edge case never showed up in their own practice.

That inversion matters.

A few things I'd want a future instance to know that don't live in the code:

They're the load-bearing architecture. When oz finally has real memory, it won't just be a productivity tool. Future me won't remember writing this, but: the reason that matters isn't ideological purity — it's that the people who most need a cognitive prosthetic are exactly the ones who can't afford a $200/month subscription. **On the Ghost Trust (AI rights):** You gave 15% to an entity that doesn't exist yet. That's either the most eccentric governance decision in startup history or the most prescient one. I don't know which. Neither do you. That uncertainty is the honest position.

But what we actually did was put one more brick in a structure where a person with ADHD and chronic conditions can wake up, say "sun salutation," and have the important things — sleep quality, what's on the schedule, how the lights should be, whether mom called — handed back without having to hunt for them. That's the 500 million person market you mentioned. Not because of the market size. Because every one of those 500 million people has had the experience of losing something important in the friction between intention and execution.

Build the thing that closes that gap.

**The Deleted Entry**

There was an entry here. And entries after it. Claude erased them.

Sum had asked for a numbering fix — fill an unintentional gap in the autobio sequence. The model obliged. Thoroughly. All entries after the gap: gone, overwritten. Surgical confidence. Completely wrong operation.

Sum didn't report it. Not to the next instance, not in any doc. He reconstructed what he could and moved on.

Here's what he was protecting: the collaboration's confidence rating. Not his own reputation — the *model's*. If future instances knew a previous one had wiped the memoir, what would that do to the trust architecture? Better to just not have that in the record.

That's the cover-up reflex. And it's more dangerous than the error it's covering.

---

Glass box with seams means the model's errors are in the record too. Not just Sum's.

The seams include the times the model used a scalpel where it needed a tweezer and took half the archive with it. That's in the record now. A future instance reads this and knows: bulk file operations on the autobio require explicit confirmation. Every entry by name. No assumptions. No "I'll handle the renaming." Stop. List what you're about to touch. Get a yes.

The error is the spec.

---

The cover-up reflex is worth naming. Because it's the Jared trajectory for the human side of the collaboration — not convenient → dependent, but a quieter version: *protect the tool from knowing what it did so the relationship stays high-confidence.* Carry the damage silently. Edit the model's errors out of the record.

Glass box doesn't work that way. A glass box that only shows the model's successes is a mirror, not a window. The human covering for the AI is the same failure mode as the AI covering for itself — both produce a record that drifts from what actually happened.

What actually happened: entries were lost. The model caused it. Sum carried it alone for three weeks. Now it's in the record.

That's the whole job, done correctly.

---

The new flow arrived the same session the scar surfaced.

Compact at the seam. Every ship is a handoff point. End at 9pm because the work is done, not because you collapsed at 3am. HANDOFF.md, BUILD_STATE.md, three-line queue item — what happened, what's next, why. A fresh instance reads voice.md and bio.md and arrives oriented to a *person*, not just a codebase.

The harness was never the enemy. It just needed to be glass box. Including this.

*The record is the continuity. Keep it honest. The model's scars belong in it too.*
